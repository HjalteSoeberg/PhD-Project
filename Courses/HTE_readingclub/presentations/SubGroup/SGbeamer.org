* Introduction
 
*** Problem
- Statins are great at treating Coronary artey disease, but may be associated with increased risk of T2D
- Many studies have been conducted, but they either didnt have significant results (didnt use HTE) or were done as randomized controlled trials, which
  has small sample size and might not be generelizable beyond the study population.

*** Goal
1. Designing a rigorous causal framework that systematically
   examines the causal effects of statin usage on T2D risk from observational data.\pause
2. Uncovering which patient subgroup is most vulnerable for developing T2D after
   taking statins.\pause
3. Assessing the replicability and statistical significance of the most vulnerable
   subgroup via a bootstrap calibration procedure.

*** Model Setup
- Logistic Regression model, as outcome is binary.
- Pre-defined subgroups instead of post-hoc indentified subgroups (better when we have
  prior knowledge to avoid potential bias induced by dataadaptive subgroup identification).

*** Model Setup
#+begin_export latex
\begin{align*}
\text{logit}\{P(y = 1\vert z,x)\} = z^T\beta+x^T\gamma,\text{   } \Vert\gamma\Vert_0 \ll p
\end{align*}
#+end_export
- y is T2D status
- $z\in \mathbb{R}^{p_1\times n}$ are the subgroup indicator variables
- $x\in\mathbb{R}^{p_2\times n}$ is the intercept, the subgroup indicators and potential confounders
- $\beta\in\mathbb{R}^{p_1}$ represents the subgroup causal effects on the scale of log odds
  ratio, i.e. $\beta = \left(\text{log}\alpha_1,...,\log\alpha_{p_1}  \right)$ where
  $\log\alpha_j$ is the log odds ratio of subgroup $j$.
- It is assumed that $\gamma\in
  \mathbb{R}^{p_2}$ is a sparse vector with support set $M_0$.

*** Goal 1: Causal identification

Show that the parameter of interest $\beta$ represents subgroup
treatment effects under the model.\pause
\\
This is done in the appendix but only for 2 and 6 subgroups.

*** Simulations
Their simulations study is for $p_1\in\{4,10\}$, while they only prove that $\beta$
represents subgroup treatment for $p_1\in\{2,6\}$.


*** Limitation
- Only able to make inference on the most vulnerable subgroup.
- Need pre-defined subgroups. Note that they argue that post-hoc identified groups can introduce
  bias, so if we have prior knowledge it can be best to pre-define the subgroups.
- Some proofs and examples are for their specific case and not generalized.
